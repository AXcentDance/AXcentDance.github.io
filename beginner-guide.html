Your First Class

Everything you need to know to feel confident on the dance floor.

What to Expect

Walking into a dance studio for the first time can be intimidating, but at AXcent Dance, we make it easy. Our community is welcoming, and everyone was a beginner once!

What to Wear

Comfort is key! Wear clothes that allow you to move freely. Gym clothes, leggings, or casual wear (jeans/t-shirt) are all fine. Avoid anything too restrictive or too loose that might get caught.

Shoes

Please bring a pair of clean indoor shoes. Street shoes are not allowed in the studio to protect the floor. If you don't have dance shoes yet, socks or comfortable sneakers are perfect for your first class.

Partner Rotation

Bachata is a social partner dance. In our classes, we highly suggest rotating partners. It helps you learn faster and meet new people. However, if you come with a partner and prefer to stay together, we understand and respect that choice.

Hygiene

Dancing is a close-contact activity. Please be mindful of your hygiene. Fresh breath (mints are great!), deodorant, and a fresh shirt go a long way in making the experience pleasant for everyone.

Dance Shoe Guide

As you progress, having the right tools makes a huge difference. Dance shoes are designed to protect your knees and allow for smooth turns.

Do I need heels?

No! Especially not for beginners. Heels are optional for followers and should only be worn once you have mastered your balance. Flat dance shoes or sneakers are often better for learning technique.

Dance Sneakers

Brands like Fuego or Taygra offer sneakers with special soles that spin easily on dance floors but look like street shoes. These are highly recommended for leaders and followers alike.

Jazz Shoes

Soft, flexible, and affordable. Jazz shoes are a great entry-level option if you want to feel the floor and have full foot articulation without breaking the bank.
